Psychological Terror & Meta-Narrative

For this horror-themed Game Jam, my team and I intentionally steered clear of clichéd jump scares and simple ghost appearances. Instead, we leaned into psychological terror with a dash of meta-narrative.

In the opening sequence, players are asked to complete a questionnaire whose questions grow progressively stranger and more unsettling, sending chills down their spine. On the next day, they enter a text-based adventure that, scene by scene, reveals its events are intrinsically linked to the White Room itself.

"Are the players truly trapped by some mysterious force, or is this sterile chamber nothing more than a manifestation of their own fractured mind—forever beyond their control?"

My Role: Narrative Writer & Designer

As the Narrative Writer and Designer for this project, I was deeply involved in crafting the psychological atmosphere. My core contributions included:

  • Narrative Writing: I wrote all the narrative text for the text-based adventure, ensuring the tone progressively became more disturbing and surreal to build tension.
  • Branching Design: I designed the branching ending paths, allowing player choices during the text-adventure to culminate in different psychological resolutions.
